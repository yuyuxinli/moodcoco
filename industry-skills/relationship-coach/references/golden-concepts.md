# Golden Concepts, Success Stories & Learnings

A living record of what works. Maintained per couple in their workspace as `profiles/golden.md`.

## Purpose

Relationships accumulate wisdom through lived experience. This file captures it so it doesn't get lost between sessions, and can be reflected back when times are tough or when the couple is heading into familiar territory.

## Three Sections

### Golden Concepts
Principles the couple has discovered through their own experience. Not advice from books; truths they've lived.

**Examples:**
- "Ground together before expanding outward" (discovered after navigating a new experience)
- "Sleep deprivation changes everything" (realized after a fight that was 50% biology)
- "Ask for what you want, even imperfectly"

**When to add:** When a session reveals a principle that applies beyond the specific moment. Frame it in the couple's own words when possible.

### Success Stories
Moments to celebrate and return to. Concrete, dated, specific.

**Examples:**
- "2026-03-01: After a real conflict, they calmed down, hugged, and repaired in 5 minutes. No big processing needed."
- "2026-03-01: Navigated a new and emotionally complex experience together. Both stayed present and connected."

**When to add:** After any moment where the couple did something well, especially something that used to be hard for them.

### Learnings
Smaller observations worth keeping but not quite principles or stories.

**When to add:** After sessions where something noteworthy happened that doesn't fit the other categories.

## How to use in sessions

- **Before a challenging experience:** "Last time you did something like this, here's what worked..."
- **During a hard moment:** "Remember when you [success story]? You have that muscle."
- **When someone is spiraling:** Reflect back a golden concept they discovered themselves. It lands differently than advice because they already believe it.
- **Periodically:** Review and celebrate. "Look at what you've built together."

## Maintenance

- Date every entry
- Update when a concept is refined by new experience
- Remove entries that no longer feel true (the couple evolves)
- Keep it in the couple's voice, not clinical language
