## 1. 5-4-3-2-1 感官着陆法 (5-4-3-2-1 Grounding Technique)

### 来源卡片（主来源：直接使用 "5-4-3-2-1" 编号的权威临床来源）

- **Title:** *5-4-3-2-1 Coping Technique for Anxiety*
- **Author:** Sara Smith, BSW, Behavioral Health Partners (BHP)
- **Institution:** University of Rochester Medical Center
- **Date:** April 2018
- **URL:** https://www.urmc.rochester.edu/behavioral-health-partners/bhp-blog/april-2018/5-4-3-2-1-coping-technique-for-anxiety.aspx

**Verbatim quote — 5-4-3-2-1 五步定义：**

> Acknowledge **FIVE** things you see around you. It could be a pen, a spot on the ceiling, anything in your surroundings.
>
> Acknowledge **FOUR** things you can touch around you. It could be your hair, a pillow, or the ground under your feet.
>
> Acknowledge **THREE** things you hear. This could be any external sound.
>
> Acknowledge **TWO** things you can smell. Maybe you are in your office and smell pencil, or maybe you are in your bedroom and smell a pillow.
>
> Acknowledge **ONE** thing you can taste. What does the inside of your mouth taste like—gum, coffee, or the sandwich from lunch?

### 来源卡片（理论基础：Grounding 技术体系）

- **Title:** *Seeking Safety: A Treatment Manual for PTSD and Substance Abuse*
- **Author:** Lisa M. Najavits, Ph.D.
- **Date:** 2002
- **Publisher:** Guilford Press
- **Verbatim source:** UCSF School of Nursing 授权改编版 PDF（见下方引用块）

### 原始来源

**该技术没有单一的、可引用的原始学术论文。** "5-4-3-2-1" 编号版本广泛出现在临床实践指南和医疗机构文档中（如 URMC 上述来源），其理论根基可追溯到两个独立的传统：

#### (a) Betty Alice Erickson 的感官自我催眠法

- **创始人**: Betty Alice Erickson (MS, LPC, LMFT)，催眠治疗先驱 Milton H. Erickson 的女儿
- **最早文字记录**: Bandler, R. & Grinder, J. (1981). *Trance-Formations: Neuro-Linguistic Programming and the Structure of Hypnosis*. Real People Press, Moab, UT.
  - Betty Erickson 的方法在该书中被描述为"The Betty Erickson Special"
- **原始形式**: 实际上是 **3-2-1 倒计数**（每轮分别注意 3/2/1 个视觉、听觉、触觉刺激），后来被临床实践者扩展为 **5-4-3-2-1** 版本
- **注意**: Betty Erickson 发明此技术时 NLP 尚不存在；它因 Bandler 将其收入书中而被归入 NLP 体系

#### (b) 创伤/PTSD 临床实践中的感官着陆

感官着陆作为一类临床技术，在创伤治疗文献中有独立的发展脉络：

- **Najavits, L.M.** (2002). *Seeking Safety: A Treatment Manual for PTSD and Substance Abuse*. Guilford Press.
  - 是 grounding 技术在 PTSD 治疗中最具影响力的系统化描述之一
  - **原文定义**（来自 UCSF 护理学院授权改编版，原文标注 "Adapted from Najavits LM. 'Seeking Safety'"）：

> **WHAT IS GROUNDING?**
>
> Grounding is a set of simple strategies to detach from emotional pain such as anger, sadness, anxiety, self-harm impulses, cravings, etc. Grounding works by focusing outward, rather than inward to self. It's also like
> - Distraction
> - Centering
> - Creating a safe place
>
> When you are overwhelmed, grounding helps to detach so that you can gain control over your feelings. Grounding 'anchors' you to the present moment.
>
> **GUIDELINES**
> - Grounding can be done any time, any place, anywhere and no one has to know.
> - Keep your eyes open, scan the room, and turn the light on to stay in touch with the present.
> - Stay neutral—no judgments of "good" and "bad."
> - Focus on the present, not the past or future.
> - Grounding is not relaxation. Grounding is active, focuses on distraction.

  *来源：UCSF School of Nursing 授权改编版 PDF，原始来源注明为 Najavits LM. "Seeking Safety." URL: https://nursing.ucsf.edu/sites/nursing.ucsf.edu/files/inline-files/Grounding.pdf*

- **SAMHSA** (2014). *Trauma-Informed Care in Behavioral Health Services*. Treatment Improvement Protocol (TIP) Series, No. 57.
  - 将着陆技术分为四类：观察聚焦、情绪调节、注意力转移、呼吸练习
  - 值得注意的是，该指南**未特别提及 5-4-3-2-1 这一具体编号形式**

- **Van der Kolk, B.** (2014). *The Body Keeps the Score: Brain, Mind, and Body in the Healing of Trauma*. Viking.
  - 强调创伤记忆以感官碎片形式（视觉、嗅觉、情感、听觉、动觉）存储，为感官着陆的理论基础提供了支持

### 唯一直接以 5-4-3-2-1 为主题的学术论文

- Imran, A. (2020). Combat Against Stress, Anxiety and Panic Attacks: 5-4-3-2-1 Coping Technique. *Journal of Traumatic Stress Disorders & Treatment*, 9(4).
  - **注意**: 这是一篇描述性文章，不是 RCT；未追溯该技术的原始出处

### 我们文档的描述是否准确？

| 项目 | 我们的描述 | 实际情况 | 是否一致 |
|------|-----------|---------|---------|
| 五感倒计数 | 5 看、4 触、3 听、2 闻、1 尝 | Betty Erickson 原版仅用视觉/听觉/触觉三个通道，且从 3 开始倒数；5-4-3-2-1 五感版本是后来的临床改编 | 部分一致 |
| 用于焦虑/恐慌 | 是 | 广泛用于焦虑、PTSD、恐慌发作 | 一致 |

### 需要注意的差异和简化

1. **编号不同**: 原版 Betty Erickson 法是 3-2-1（三个感官通道），不是 5-4-3-2-1（五个感官通道）。5-4-3-2-1 版本是后来的临床改编，目前**无法追溯到某篇特定论文**
2. **嗅觉和味觉是后加的**: 原版只涉及视觉、听觉、触觉（体感）
3. **引用建议**: 如需标注来源，最诚实的表述是"改编自 Betty Alice Erickson 的感官着陆自我催眠法（Bandler & Grinder, 1981），后在临床实践中扩展为五感版本"

## 2. 循环叹息 / 生理性叹息 (Physiological Sigh / Cyclic Sighing)

### 原始来源

该技术涉及两层学术文献：(a) 生理性叹息的神经科学发现，(b) 将其转化为呼吸训练方案的临床研究。

#### (a) 生理性叹息的神经机制发现

- **Li, P., Janczewski, W.A., Yackle, K., Kam, K., Pagliardini, S., Krasnow, M.A., & Feldman, J.L.** (2016). The peptidergic control circuit for sighing. *Nature*, 530(7590), 293-297. DOI: 10.1038/nature16964
  - UCLA (Feldman 实验室) 和 Stanford (Krasnow 实验室) 合作
  - 发现控制叹息的神经回路位于 preBötzinger Complex (preBötC)
  - 揭示了 neuromedin B (Nmb) 和 gastrin-releasing peptide (Grp) 两种神经肽在诱发叹息中的作用
  - **这是关于叹息的神经科学基础研究，不是呼吸训练方案**

#### (b) 循环叹息呼吸训练的临床研究（核心引用）

- **Balban, M.Y., Neri, E., Kogon, M.M., Weed, L., Nouriani, B., Jo, B., Holl, G., Zeitzer, J.M., Spiegel, D., & Huberman, A.D.** (2023). Brief structured respiration practices enhance mood and reduce physiological arousal. *Cell Reports Medicine*, 4(1), 100895. DOI: 10.1016/j.xcrm.2022.100895
  - Stanford 大学，David Spiegel 和 Andrew Huberman 共同领导
  - **研究设计**: 随机对照试验，111 名健康志愿者，28 天，每天 5 分钟
  - **对比条件**: 循环叹息 vs 箱式呼吸 vs 循环过度通气 vs 正念冥想
  - **核心结果**: 循环叹息在改善正面情绪和降低呼吸频率方面显著优于正念冥想
  - **这是支持"循环叹息"作为临床干预的最权威 RCT**

### 论文摘要（Verbatim Abstract）

以下为从 PubMed / Cell Reports Medicine 获取的摘要核心内容（原文英语）：

> Controlled breathwork practices have emerged as potential tools for stress management and well-being. This remote, randomized, controlled study (NCT05304000) compared three different daily 5-minute breathwork exercises with mindfulness meditation over one month. The breathing conditions included cyclic sighing (prolonged exhalations), box breathing (equal duration inhalations/retentions/exhalations), and cyclic hyperventilation with retention (longer inhalations/shorter exhalations). Breathwork, especially the exhale-focused cyclic sighing, produces greater improvement in mood (p < 0.05) and reduction in respiratory rate (p < 0.05) compared with mindfulness meditation. Daily 5-min cyclic sighing has promise as an effective stress management exercise.

*来源：PubMed ID 36630953 / Cell Reports Medicine, DOI: 10.1016/j.xcrm.2022.100895*

### 论文中描述的呼吸方案（Protocol, Verbatim）

以下为 Stanford Medicine News 引用的论文原文指导语（来源：https://med.stanford.edu/news/insights/2023/02/cyclic-sighing-can-help-breathe-away-anxiety.html）：

> "Breathe in through your nose. When you've comfortably filled your lungs, take a second, deeper sip of air to expand your lungs as much as possible. Then, very slowly, exhale through your mouth until all the air is gone."

论文同时指出应重复练习约五分钟（"for about five minutes"）。

### 论文关键结论（Key Findings, Verbatim）

Stanford Medicine News 引用论文结果的原文表述：

> "All groups reported lower anxiety and a better mood, and the controlled breathing groups reported significantly greater increases in energy, joy and peacefulness, rising an average of 1.91 points above baseline on the 'good feelings' scale."

> "Cyclic sighers reported the greatest daily improvements, an effect that increased over time."

研究者 David Spiegel, MD 原话：

> "We may be able to identify certain kinds of anxiety that respond substantially to this simple treatment."

*来源：Stanford Medicine News, Feb 2023. https://med.stanford.edu/news/insights/2023/02/cyclic-sighing-can-help-breathe-away-anxiety.html*

### 我们文档的描述是否准确？

| 项目 | 我们的描述 | 论文原文 | 是否一致 |
|------|-----------|---------|---------|
| 双吸气 + 长呼气 | 鼻吸 4 秒 + 追加吸 2 秒 + 嘴呼 8 秒 | 未指定具体秒数，仅描述"缓慢吸气 → 追加吸气 → 缓慢完全呼气" | **不一致** |
| 重复次数 | 3 轮 | 持续 5 分钟（不限轮数） | **不一致** |
| 来源归属 | Stanford 研究 | 确实是 Stanford (Spiegel & Huberman) | 一致 |

### 需要注意的差异和简化

1. **具体秒数是我们自行添加的**: 论文（Balban et al., 2023）**不包含 "吸 4 秒 + 追加 2 秒 + 呼 8 秒" 这一计时方案**。论文只描述了动作模式（双吸气 + 长呼气），未规定时长。Huberman Lab 播客/Newsletter 中同样未给出精确秒数
2. **3 轮 vs 5 分钟**: 论文方案是每日练习 5 分钟（重复多个循环）。Huberman 在其播客中提到实时减压仅需 1-3 次叹息即可。我们的"3 轮"既不匹配论文（5 分钟持续练习）也不匹配 Huberman 的建议（1-3 次）
3. **引用建议**: 应引用 Balban et al. (2023) 作为核心来源，但需注明具体计时方案（4-2-8 秒）是教学简化，非论文原始方案。如需给用户明确的计时指导，可以保留 4-2-8 作为"建议节奏"，但不应声称这是"Stanford 研究的方案"

## 3. 危机干预流程 (Crisis Intervention Protocol)

### 来源卡片（主标准：QPR）

- **Title:** *QPR Gatekeeper Training for Suicide Prevention: The Model, Rationale and Theory*
- **Author:** Paul G. Quinnett, Ph.D.
- **Date:** 2007 (QPR 模型创建于 1995)
- **Institution:** QPR Institute, Spokane, WA
- **URL:** https://qprinstitute.com/about-qpr
- **证据等级:** SAMHSA National Registry of Evidence-based Programs and Practices 认证

### 我们文档的五步流程

1. 停下所有事情 (stop everything)
2. 确认对方感受 (acknowledge)
3. 直接询问"你安全吗？" (ask "are you safe?")
4. 提供热线电话 (provide hotline)
5. 陪伴在场 (stay present)

### 最匹配的已有标准框架

#### (a) QPR — Question, Persuade, Refer【**主要参考标准 PRIMARY STANDARD**】

- **创始人**: Paul G. Quinnett, Ph.D.（临床心理学家，华盛顿大学医学院精神病学与行为科学系临床助理教授）
- **创建时间**: 1995 年
- **机构**: QPR Institute（1999 年正式成立为独立组织）。官网：https://qprinstitute.com/about-qpr
- **核心理论文献**: Quinnett, P.G. (2007). QPR Gatekeeper Training for Suicide Prevention: The Model, Rationale and Theory. QPR Institute, Spokane, WA. (可通过 ResearchGate 获取)
- **相关著作**: Quinnett, P.G. (1987). *Suicide: The Forever Decision*. Crossroad Publishing. ISBN: 978-0824513528
- **证据等级**: SAMHSA National Registry of Evidence-based Programs and Practices 认证项目
- **覆盖面**: 截至 2015 年已培训超过 100 万名美国公民作为 QPR 守门人

**QPR 三个步骤官方定义**（来源：QPR Institute 官网 + Wellness in the Woods 培训资料，https://mnwitw.org/questions-persuade-and-refer-qpr）：

> **QPR stands for Question, Persuade, and Refer — the 3 simple steps anyone can learn to help save a life from suicide.**

**Question（询问）**：

> Plan a time and place to ask the question.
>
> - "Have you been very unhappy lately?"
> - "Have you ever wanted to stop living?"
> - "Are you thinking about suicide?"

**Persuade（劝说）**：

> Listen to what they have to say.
>
> - "Will you go with me to see a counselor?"
> - "Will you let me help you make an appointment?"
> - "Will you promise me...?"

**Refer（转介）**：

> Encourage a referral.
>
> - Take them to a professional yourself.
> - Hold them accountable to see a professional and keep the appointment.
> - Encourage them to accept help, even if it is for the future.

*来源：Wellness in the Woods / Minnesota QPR Training. https://mnwitw.org/questions-persuade-and-refer-qpr；QPR Institute. https://qprinstitute.com/about-qpr*

#### (b) #BeThe1To 五步模型 (National Suicide Prevention Lifeline / 988)【**辅助参考标准**】

- **来源**: 988 Suicide and Crisis Lifeline / Vibrant Emotional Health
- **官方网站**: https://bethe1to.com/
- **理论基础**: Thomas Joiner 的人际心理理论 (Interpersonal-Psychological Theory of Suicide) 和 Klonsky & May 的三步理论 (Three-Step Theory)

**五个步骤官方定义**（来源：Hawaii CARES 988 / NIMH 官方文件，https://hicares.hawaii.gov/2023/09/5-steps-to-help-someone-in-crisis/）：

**Ask（询问）**：

> "Asking the question 'Are you thinking about suicide?' communicates that you're open to speaking about suicide in a non-judgmental and supportive way."

**Be There（陪伴）**：

> "This could mean being physically present for someone, speaking with them on the phone when you can, or any other way that shows support for the person at risk."

**Help Keep Them Safe（保障安全）**：

> "After the 'Ask' step, and you've determined suicide is indeed being talked about, it's important to find out a few things to establish immediate safety."

**Help Them Connect（帮助连接）**：

> "Helping someone with thoughts of suicide connect with ongoing supports (like the 988 Lifeline) can help them establish a safety net for those moments they find themselves in a crisis."

**Follow Up（后续跟进）**：

> "After your initial contact with a person experiencing thoughts of suicide, and after you've connected them with the immediate support systems they need, make sure to follow-up with them to see how they're doing."

NIMH 官方精简版（来源：https://www.nimh.nih.gov/health/publications/5-action-steps-to-help-someone-having-thoughts-of-suicide）：

> **ASK:** "Are you thinking about suicide?" It's not an easy question to ask, but it can help start a conversation.
>
> **BE THERE:** "Listening without judgment is key to learning what the person is thinking and feeling."
>
> **HELP KEEP THEM SAFE:** "Reducing access to highly lethal items or places can help prevent suicide."
>
> **HELP THEM CONNECT:** "Connecting the person with the 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline (call or text 988) and other community resources can give them a safety net when they need it."
>
> **FOLLOW UP:** "Staying in touch with the person after they have experienced a crisis or been discharged from care can make a difference."

#### (c) ASIST — Applied Suicide Intervention Skills Training

- **开发机构**: LivingWorks
- **形式**: 2 天培训项目
- **特点**: 提供从初始接触到后续跟进的完整自杀急救干预模型
- **核心环节**: 积极参与、建立联系、协作解决问题、风险评估、安全计划、安全计划跟进

#### (d) SAFE-T — Suicide Assessment Five-Step Evaluation and Triage

- **来源**: SAMHSA
- **五个步骤**: 识别风险因素和保护因素 → 自杀评估问询 → 确定风险等级和干预措施 → 制定治疗计划 → 记录文档

### 我们文档的描述是否匹配已有框架？

| 我们的步骤 | QPR | #BeThe1To | 匹配度 |
|-----------|-----|-----------|-------|
| 1. 停下所有事情 | (隐含) | (隐含) | 我们独有的明确步骤 |
| 2. 确认感受 | Persuade 中的同理心倾听 | Be There | 部分匹配 |
| 3. 问"你安全吗？" | Question (直接询问自杀意念) | Ask + Help Keep Them Safe | 部分匹配 |
| 4. 提供热线 | Refer | Help Them Connect | 匹配 |
| 5. 陪伴在场 | (隐含) | Be There + Follow Up | 部分匹配 |

### 需要注意的差异和简化

1. **我们的流程是多个标准框架的综合简化版，不完全等同于任何一个**。最接近的是 #BeThe1To 五步模型，但步骤顺序和侧重点有所不同
2. **QPR 的"Question"是直接问"你是否在考虑自杀？"**，而我们的"你安全吗？"更温和。两种提问方式在不同框架中都被使用。研究显示直接询问自杀意念并不会增加风险（反而可能降低）
3. **"停下所有事情"是我们添加的步骤**，在标准框架中通常是隐含的前提而非独立步骤。这在 AI 陪伴产品语境下是合理的补充（AI 需要明确切换到危机模式）
4. **引用建议**: 可以表述为"参考 QPR (Quinnett, 1995) 和 #BeThe1To (National Suicide Prevention Lifeline) 等循证框架设计"，而非声称遵循某个特定标准
